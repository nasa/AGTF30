T-MATS: Power Generator Library Block

Purpose

This block can be used to convert a power demand with rotational speed into a torque. In general, this block is used to simulate a simple power generator with no dynamics.

Background

To compute the torque, this block utilizes the equation below, along with a few unit conversions. $$ Torq = \frac{-PowerDMD * 5252}{\textit{Eff_M} * Nmech * 746}$$ In the above equation, 5252 is used to convert from HP/RPM to ft/lbf and 746 is used to convert HP to Watts.

Instructions

To use this block:

  • Connect the input power demand and shaft speed to the corresponding places on the block.
  • Connect the calculated torque to the next block in your simulation.
  • Specify the efficiency by double clicking on the block.

Potential Errors

When using this block, an error will occur if either Eff_M or Nmech is equal to zero. To avoid this error, ensure that both the efficiency and shaft speed inputs are nonzero.
